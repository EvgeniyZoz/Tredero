Trade Stocks with Tredero

Stock trading is a popular investment option for both retail and institutional investors. Stock trading entails purchasing a company's stock to profit from a rise in the stock price or selling a company's stock in the hopes of profiting from a decrease in the stock price.

Shares are units of ownership in a company, with the owner receiving an equal share of any profits made by the company. If you possess 100 shares of a firm with 1,000 issued shares, you will be entitled to ten per cent of the company's profits.

While stock trading is extremely popular among investors, many traders find the high capital requirements and minimal leverage offered by stockbrokers prohibitive. Stock CFD trading has grown in popularity as a result. A Contract for Difference, for example, does not require physical ownership of, say, Tesla stock. Stock CFDs are an agreement between a buyer and a seller to swap the price difference in Tesla stock at a specific period.

Stocks Leveraging

Stock CFDs are made on the value of 100 shares in any specific company. Stock CFDs provide several advantages over traditional transactions, one of which is the higher leverage available. Leverage in stock trading, like any other trade, can magnify losses as well as profits, so proceed with caution when using leverage.

Take, for example, Google stock. Google's stock is currently trading at around $2,400. This is for a single share! Owning Google shares would necessitate a large amount of cash. At leverage of 1:50, a full contract of Google stock would only require $4,800 in available funds in the case of leveraged CFD trades.

What influences Stock Price

Several things influence stock prices. Earnings, for example, have a significant impact on stock prices as well as new product launches. The roll-out of COVID-19 vaccinations is an example of this, with pharmaceutical companies that have effective vaccines seeing considerable benefits in their stock values. Apple is a prime illustration of this phenomena, with stock prices skyrocketing whenever the tech giant introduces a new iPhone model.
